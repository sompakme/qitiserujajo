    What should someone look for in a comfortable standing shoe?

    Lobkova tells Bazaar her patients benefit from shoes that feature a proper cushion, a firm arch support, and a 6- to 8- millimeter heel-to-toe gradient. A heel-to-toe gradient, also called an offset, is the difference in height between the heel and the ball of the foot. “Ideally, the heel should look about one inch higher than the ball of the foot,” she says. “This ensures a slightly elevated heel, so that there is reduced tension on the Achilles tendon.” The doctor also shared that the offset helps maintain proper posture by reducing pressure on the lower back.

    As for the insole of the shoe, there should be a sturdy medial and lateral arch support to reduce tension on the plantar fascia. “The plantar fascia band is commonly aggravated or overstretched while standing for long periods of time,” she explains. “The insole should also have a deep heel cup for heel and ankle stability.”

    What type of shoe is best for standing all day?

    Lobkova says people who find themselves on their feet all day should consider sneakers with a supple mesh or leather upper, a foam midsole, and a rubber outsole. She also notes that the heel counter (the part of the sneaker surrounding the back of the heel) should be firm.

    To check for firmness, she recommends applying what she likes to call a pinch test. “[When you] pinch the sides of the counter, the material should not be flexible. It should be firm, and it should not collapse in,” she explains.
