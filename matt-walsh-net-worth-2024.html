    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Matt Walsh was born in Chicago, Illinois, and attended Northern Illinois University. He started his career in comedy in the late 1980s and early 1990s, performing stand-up and improv in Chicago. In 1991, he co-founded the Upright Citizens Brigade (UCB), a sketch comedy and improv group that would later become a significant force in the comedy world.

    Establishing the Upright Citizens Brigade

    Walsh moved to New York City with the UCB in 1996. The group quickly gained popularity, leading to a sketch show on Comedy Central that ran from 1998 to 2000. The UCB also opened their own theater and training center, which has become a breeding ground for some of the most successful comedians in the industry.

    Acting Career

    Walsh has had a prolific acting career, appearing in over 100 films and television shows. He is best known for his role as Mike McLintock on the HBO series “Veep,” which earned him two Primetime Emmy nominations. His other notable roles include parts in “The Hangover,” “Ted,” and “Old School.”
