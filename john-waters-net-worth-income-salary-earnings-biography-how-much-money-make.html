    He was born on April 22, 1946, in Los Angeles, California, and has worked as a film director and screenwriter, as an actor and stand-up comedian, as a journalist, as a visual artist, and as an art collector. He first gained prominence in the early 1970s for his provocative cult films. Waters’ garbage films from the 1970s and early 1980s are populated by his regular troupe of performers known as the Dreamlanders, which includes Divine, Mink Stole, David Lochary, Mary Vivian Pearce, and Edith Massey, among other actors. Since Desperate Living (1977), director John Waters has cast real-life convicted criminals (Liz Renay, Patty Hearst) and notable persons (such as John Wayne Gacy) in his films (Traci Lords, a former pornographic actress). In 1988, Waters tried his hand at popular filmmaking with Hairspray, which starred Ricki Lake and grossed a modest $8 million at the box office in the United States. When Hairspray was first released in 2002, it was transformed into a long-running Broadway musical, which in turn was translated into a successful musical film that grossed more than $200 million globally. Several familiar actors and celebrities, such as Johnny Depp, Edward Furlong, Melanie Griffith, Chris Isaak, Johnny Knoxville, Martha Plimpton and Christina Ricci appeared in Waters’ films following the success of the original film version of Hairspray. Waters’ films also featured the voices of actresses such as Lili Taylor, Kathleen Turner, and Tracey Ullman. Despite the fact that he has residences in New York City and San Francisco, as well as a vacation house in Provincetown, Waters still mostly dwells in Baltimore, Maryland, the setting for all of his films. His pencil moustache, which he has had since the early 1970s, distinguishes him from the crowd. He has been in the business for himself.
