    Education

    Clay is a Cleveland, Ohio, native who graduated from Benedictine High School. He subsequently graduated from George Washington University with a degree in International Relations in 2015.

    Career

    Karpeh released his debut single, “Cold War,” in 2017. The song was a surprise hit, amassing over 100 million Spotify streams. He then released the EPs Blood Type (2018) and Resonance (2018). He released the song “Swim Home” in 2019, which is included on the soundtrack for the third season of the Netflix series 13 Reasons Why.

    Karpeh, the gifted musician, released his debut studio album, Deadpan Love, in 2021. This musical masterpiece was lauded by critics, particularly Billboard, who called it an “amazing debut.” 

    The journal praised Karpeh’s excellent ability to blend numerous genres smoothly and his ability to create seductive melodies that linger with listeners long after the music has ended.

     In 2022, Karpeh delighted his fans by releasing his highly anticipated EP Thin Ice on the Cake. Building on his growing popularity, he has had the incredible opportunity to collaborate with renowned artists like John Mayer, Taylor Swift, and Jack Antonoff.
