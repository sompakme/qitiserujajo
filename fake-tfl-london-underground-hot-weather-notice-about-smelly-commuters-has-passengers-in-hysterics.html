    Fake TfL London Underground hot weather notice about 'smelly' commuters has passengers in hysterics

    Anyone who's braved the Tube this week to get to an air conditioned office will know all too well that the London Underground network hasn't been the most comfortable place to be during the heatwave. Commuters faced suspended and reduced services due to unprecedented temperatures.

    18 Sep 2024, 00:00

    Anyone who's braved the Tube this week to get to an air conditioned office will know all too well that the London Underground network hasn't been the most comfortable place to be during the heatwave. Commuters faced suspended and reduced services due to unprecedented temperatures.

    This resulted in huge crowds forming in stations like London St Pancras, Green Park, Euston station and many others despite many people choosing to work from home during the heatwave. There were 1.06 million entries and exits made by London Underground passengers up to 10am on Monday - down 18 per cent compared with the same period week before.

    However, this didn't stop rush hour Tube journeys being packed and sweaty. It inspired a fake notice, parodying TFL' s regular notices in train stations which has had Londoners' in hysterics.

    READ MORE: London Underground: Northern Line Tube driver so funny commuters stay on train past their stop

    The fake weather alert read: "Please carry a bottle of water with you at all times. You can use it to beat yourself to death with when the heat becomes too much. To avoid flooded carriages, make sure to direct all your perspiration into the provided sweat-gutters. There is a high possibility most passengers will smell like some prawns that were abandoned in an alleyway six months ago."

    The post engaged many under the trending hashtag, heatwave which documented what has been called the hottest day of the year. One tweeted: "Hysterical love it. Seriously though the Underground must be worse than a train and bus too. The next tweet I see where people are saying 'I love the heat' or about time summer got here...I will not be responsible for my actions."

    "Brilliant! I can only imagine how rotten it must be on a busy train in this heat," another tweeted as passengers were most likely packed in like a pack of sardines. And as many Londoners and tourists have been giving advice on how to stay cool during the heatwave, one person offered their advice to get some respite while on a hot train. "I used to carry a bottle of frozen water to put on my head worked like a treat", they tweeted.

    While the heatwave has brought a sense of doom and gloom for many, it has also offered a dream-like holiday to some. Many people attended UK beaches while some sought refuge in their homes. Regardless, this debates the speculation around whether such high temperatures could be the new normal in the future.

    In a televised interview with Sky News, the Mayor of London, Sadiq Khan said: "This is not a normal temperature in our city or our country. 40 degrees is not normal which is one of the consequences of climate change. We have to adapt now because this may become the norm rather than the exception."
